Grandmaster Chess

The classic game of strategy, tactics, and foresight.

info Overview

Chess is a two-player strategy board game played on a checkered board with 64 squares arranged in an 8√ó8 grid. Each player begins with 16 pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two knights, two bishops, and eight pawns. The objective is to checkmate the opponent's king by placing it under an inescapable threat of capture.

grid_view Game Setup

The board is placed so that a white square is in each player's near-right corner. Pieces are set up on the two ranks closest to each player.

  • Pawns occupy the second rank.
  • Rooks occupy the corners.
  • Knights are placed next to the rooks.
  • Bishops are placed next to the knights.
  • Queen is placed on her own color (white queen on white square, black queen on black square).
  • King occupies the remaining square.
lightbulb

Remember: "White on right" for the corner square, and "Queen on her color" for initial placement.

stars Special Moves

Castling

A defensive move where the King moves two squares towards a Rook, and the Rook hops over the King. This is the only time two pieces move in one turn. Conditions apply (e.g., neither piece has moved, path is clear, king is not in check).

En Passant

A special pawn capture that can only occur immediately after a pawn makes a move of two squares from its starting square, and it could have been captured by an enemy pawn had it advanced only one square.

Promotion

When a pawn reaches the eighth rank (the other side of the board), it must be exchanged for the player's choice of a queen, rook, bishop, or knight of the same color.

emoji_events Winning Conditions

Checkmate

The primary objective. The opponent's king is under threat of capture (check) and has no legal move to escape the threat.

Draw / Stalemate

The game ends in a tie if the player whose turn it is has no legal move and is not in check (Stalemate), or by agreement, 3-fold repetition, or 50-move rule.
